OCEAN ENERGY MISSION

Energy Harvesting from Sea Waves at Puri Sea Beach

Energy Generated: 0 kW

1. Introduction

This project aims to harness renewable ocean wave energy and convert it into usable electrical power through mechanical transmission and generator systems.

2. Objective of the Project

To design a sustainable, cyclone-resistant ocean wave energy conversion system deployed at Puri Sea Beach, Odisha.

3. Site Selection: Puri Sea Beach

Puri Sea Beach is selected due to consistent wave activity, coastal accessibility, renewable energy potential, and grid connectivity options.

4. Principle of Operation

Wave force → Translational motion → Rotational conversion → Generator → Electricity generation.

5. System Design and Components

Wave Plate, Steel Rod, Pipe Structure, Gear Mechanism, Generator, Energy Storage Unit.

6. Working of the System

Wave motion pushes submerged plate → rod displacement → mechanical transmission → rotational motion → generator produces electricity.

7. Safety Measures & Cyclone Protection

Corrosion-resistant materials, deep anchoring, reinforced housing, shock absorbers, storm-lock mechanism.

8. Energy Loss & Efficiency

Mechanical loss ~15%, transmission loss ~10%, generator efficiency ~85%. Estimated overall system efficiency: 65–70%.

9. Integration with Existing Power System

Generated power → Inverter → Transformer → Connected to coastal grid infrastructure.

10. Advantages

Renewable source, zero carbon emission, sustainable coastal deployment, long operational life.

11. Limitations & Future Scope

High installation cost, marine corrosion. Future scope includes AI-based efficiency optimization and modular floating systems.

12. Conclusion

Ocean wave energy offers a promising sustainable solution for renewable coastal electricity generation.

13. Overall Budget

Prototype Cost: ₹8–12 Lakhs
Full Scale Deployment: ₹2–5 Crores (capacity dependent).
